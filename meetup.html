Visualization and Mapping for Autonomous Vehicles

Overview

On Wednesday, March 27, I attended a meetup at Uber headquarters. The event was part of the Uber Visualization Nights series put on by the Engineering org at Uber. At these talks, Uber engineers discuss things they're working on relating to data visualization, and then outside guests are given the opportunity to present their work in the space.

All of the software being discussed is open source and there was a general spirit of cooperation and collaboration at the event. The presenters and their topics were:

  • Uber engineers: AVS.auto
  • Mapbox product manager: Vision SDK
  • Cruise engineers: Worldview and Webviz

If you are interested in similar meetups, you should look at the Uber Engineering Events meetup page. You can also learn about more work coming from their team at the Uber Engineering Blog. The meetings are irregular but generally happen around once a month on average.

Uber

The Uber engineers discussed AVS.auto, which is an open standard for autonomous vehicle data visualization. They have built a set of tools that allow for 3D data visualization in a web application.

Mapbox

A PM at Mapbox discussed the work that the company does in general with collecting and utilizing mapping data, then gave a brief overview of a new SDK that uses augmented reality to layer that data on top of real-time images using a mobile device.

Cruise

Engineers from Cruise, another company working on autonomous vehicles in San Francisco, presented more browser-based applications they have built to allow for AV data visualization. This included some very interesting live coding by one of them.
